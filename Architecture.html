Architecture

The interconnected hardware design

Module Connections

Every module is connected to the main backplate through an integrated connection point. This makes the module system robust, quick, and efficient at changing out modules.

The integrated connection point consists of wooden pegs and a DS-9 connector held in place by magnetic latches. The DS-9 connectors are wired to a central 3.3V rail and ground for power, while its SCL and SDA connectors all connect to the Master Module for communicating through the I2C Protocol. The DS-9 connectors were chosen for their low insertion force and availability of pins for various functions, while the magnetic latches ensure a stable and solid connection that can be disconnected easily.

Additionally, every column of modules are connected to each other through a non-permanent dovetail joint. The asymmetry in the joint designates a module as a "top" or "bottom" module. Each each module is completely swappable with another module of its kind (i.e. a bottom module can be replaced with another module), which allows for variation in difficulty and challenges.
